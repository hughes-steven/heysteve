// The Problem

Every manufacturer has the same story. There's a process running on spreadsheets, whiteboards, and tribal knowledge. The people closest to the problem already know what the solution looks like. They've been sketching it for years. Until now, building it meant six figures and six months with a dev shop. That's changed.

// What Changed

AI development tools have matured quickly. The latest generation, called Agentic Engineering, can plan, architect, test, debug, and iterate. These tools follow real engineering practices and produce documented, maintainable, production-grade software. And they get dramatically better every month.

What this means in practice: your process engineer, your ops manager, your quality lead can build real, working software with the right guidance. Tools your business actually runs on, built by the people who understand the work best.

// What We Do

You've probably been thinking about a tool that would make a real difference in how your shop runs. A quoting system. A scheduling board. A quality tracker. A production dashboard. Maybe you've been sketching it out for years.

That's where we come in. We work alongside you over eight weeks to build it. We train your team to understand, maintain, and improve it going forward. Then we hand you the keys.

// About

We're a two-person partnership based in Kitchener, Ontario. We've been building software together for almost ten years, and between us we've spent over three decades in industrial settings: shop floors, engineering rooms, sales calls, and production lines.

Steven Hughes. Twenty-plus years across the electrical, energy, and manufacturing industries, including nine years specializing in Canadian nuclear, hydro, solar, wind, and oil & gas sectors. The last six as co-founder and VP at Boardera Software, most recently as Product Manager. He brings deep intuition for the kinds of tools manufacturing teams actually adopt and run with.

Dave Workman. Chief Architect and co-founder at Boardera Software. Over a decade as a lead software developer, and co-inventor on four granted U.S. patents covering automated manufacturing analysis.
